J. Cole's Verse on 21 Savage's a lot | Analyzing the Genius Behind the Lyrics

In the world of music, lyrics hold immense power. They possess the ability to evoke emotions, tell stories, and even make powerful statements. J. Cole, a renowned rapper, demonstrates this in his verse on 21 Savage’s hit song “a lot”. In this article, we delve into the intricate details of J. Cole’s verse, dissecting the genius behind his lyrics.

Introduction: Unveiling the Essence of the Verse#

J. Cole opens his verse with a bold statement, questioning the authenticity of artists who fake their success through streaming numbers. He remarks, “I love [__] like that question, how many faking these streams? Gettin’ their plays from machines.” Through these lines, Cole challenges the mainstream music industry, shedding light on the manipulation that occurs behind the scenes.

Key Points:#

  1. Questioning the Illusion of Success: Cole aptly points out that not all artists enjoying fame and recognition are as significant as they seem. He asserts that success should be derived from genuine talent and hard work, rather than artificial means.

  2. Staying True to Himself: Cole emphasizes his own character by stating, “I don’t want no more comparisons, this is a marathon and I’m aware.” He highlights his focus on his own journey rather than engaging in unnecessary competition with other artists.

  3. The Music Must Speak: The rapper expresses his disappointment in the lack of recognition that his music receives. He had hoped that his art would speak for itself. This highlights the importance of creating music with sincerity and substance.

  4. Collaboration with 21 Savage: Cole reveals the story behind his collaboration with 21 Savage. He was approached by Savage himself, who offered him a spot on a new record. This anecdote showcases the camaraderie between the two artists and their mutual respect for one another.

  5. Reflecting on the Struggle: Through his lyrics, Cole takes a moment to reflect on his journey to success. He wonders if the sacrifices and challenges he faced were worth it, demonstrating vulnerability and introspection.

  6. Not Letting Doubt Define Him: The rapper acknowledges the doubters and haters, but remains unfazed. He highlights his ability to persevere and overcome obstacles, proving that he is stronger than any negativity thrown his way.

  7. Acknowledging the Fans: Cole sends a message to his fans, expressing gratitude and appreciation for their support. He recognizes their loyalty and assures them that he sees and values their presence in his life.

Analyzing Each Verse Line-by-Line:#

Verse 1:

  • “I love [__] like that question, how many faking these streams?”

    • In this line, Cole sets the tone for the rest of his verse by directly addressing the issue of fake success in the music industry. He questions the prevalence of streaming manipulation and raises awareness about its negative impact.

  • “I’m on the money routine, I don’t want smoke, I want cream”

    • Here, the rapper emphasizes his focus on financial success and stability. He aspires to earn money legitimately, rather than engaging in unnecessary conflict or drama.

Verse 2:

  • “No problem, I’ll show up on everyone, you know what the outcome will be”

    • Cole asserts his confidence and belief in his own skills. He states that he will consistently deliver exceptional performances that leave no room for doubt among his audience.

  • “Just gotta the point baby, rappers don’t even like rapping with me”

    • In this line, the rapper acknowledges his own abilities as a lyricist. He suggests that other artists may feel inferior or threatened by his skills, which ultimately prevents them from collaborating with him.

Verse 3:

  • “I pray for my kill ‘cause they [__] up the shot, just want you to know that you got it”

    • Cole expresses his concern for those who may have missed opportunities or made mistakes. He encourages them to keep going, assuring them that they possess the potential for greatness.

  • “I never met you but I know that you’re special, whenever you’re here, don’t doubt it”

    • Here, the rapper sends a heartfelt message to his fans. He believes that each individual possesses unique qualities and capabilities, urging them to recognize their own worth and never doubt themselves.

Outro:

  • “How much money you got? A lot. How many problems you got? A lot.”
    • In the concluding lines, Cole poses introspective questions to his audience. He prompts them to reflect on their own lives and the balance between material wealth and personal struggles.

Conclusion: The Power of Authentic Lyrics#

J. Cole’s verse on 21 Savage’s “a lot” showcases his prowess as a rapper and his ability to convey meaningful messages through his lyrics. The key points revealed in this analysis highlight his deep understanding of the music industry, his personal growth, and his commitment to staying true to himself. By questioning the notion of success and acknowledging his fans, Cole ignites an introspective journey within his listeners.

Through his thought-provoking lines, J. Cole urges his audience to embrace their uniqueness and strive for authenticity. His verse reminds us of the impact that lyrics can have on our lives, encouraging us to pause, reflect, and appreciate the genius behind the words.
